Barrel Intake & Receiving

Ordering strategy, receiving inspection procedures, storage requirements, and TTB compliance documentation.

Ordering Strategy

Loose vs. Palletized

ConfigurationBarrels per LoadConsiderations
Loose (floor-loaded)88–92 (53' trailer)Maximum density; requires hand-rolling at origin and destination. Higher labor cost.
Palletized (custom pallet)80 (53' trailer)Forklift-ready at destination. Preferred for facilities with forklift access. Slightly lower density.
Racked (full barrels)42 per load21 racks, 2 barrels each, single-layer. ~22,000 lbs. Standard for full barrel shipments.
Cost Consideration
Loose loading maximizes per-load barrel count and is preferred for large empty barrel orders when you have adequate receiving labor. Palletized is preferred when forklift time is more available than floor labor.

Pre-Arrival Checklist

  • Confirm delivery appointment with carrier at least 48 hours in advance
  • Verify dock or ground-level receiving area is clear and accessible
  • Confirm TTB permit and TIB (Transfer in Bond) documentation is in order for bonded spirits
  • Have barrel numbering system and intake log ready prior to truck arrival
  • Stage forklift (if palletized) or receiving crew (if loose) in advance

Receiving Inspection

Inspect every barrel during or immediately after unloading:

  • Bung condition: Verify bung is seated and undamaged. Re-bung immediately if loose or missing.
  • Head integrity: Check both barrel heads for cracks, splits, or significant damage.
  • Stave condition: Note any staves with visible splits, spalling, or deep checks.
  • Leak check: Place suspect barrels bung-down on a pallet and observe for 30 minutes before stacking.
  • Quantity count: Confirm barrel count against bill of lading before driver departs.

Storage Requirements

  • Orientation: Full barrels — bung to the side (3 o’clock or 9 o’clock position). Empty barrels — bung up or bung down per storage preference.
  • Stacking: Do not stack full barrels more than 2 high without barrel racks. Empty barrels may be palletized higher with adequate strapping.
  • Temperature: Store in a stable-temperature environment. Avoid areas with extreme heat cycling which accelerates evaporation and can crack heads.
  • Leak mitigation: Place a pallet or catch pan beneath any barrel with a known slow leak. Tag and monitor daily.

TTB Compliance Documentation

  • Log each barrel intake in your production records system with serial number, origin, proof, and volume
  • For MGP (MGPI) sourced barrels: complete the CBARF form and retain for records
  • For Transfer in Bond (TIB): verify TIB paperwork matches actual barrel count and proof gallons received
  • Discrepancies between BOL and actual count must be documented and reported per TTB requirements
