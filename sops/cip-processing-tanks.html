Standard Operating Procedure: Processing Tank CIP

Clean-in-place procedure for large stainless steel processing vessels.

Purpose: Establish a standardized, tiered process for cleaning stainless steel processing tanks using an alkaline detergent and an acid rinse. This ensures product purity, prevents cross-contamination, and extends equipment lifespan without over-using chemicals when a simple hot water flush will suffice.

Scope: Applies to all [Company Name] employees responsible for operating or cleaning stainless steel processing tanks.

⚠️ Safety & PPE Requirements

Vapor / Flash Hazard
An empty tank that recently held flammable liquid is full of flammable vapor. Do not open the manway until a cold water purge has been completed to knock down the vapor and dilute any remaining liquid to below 5% ABV.
Implosion Risk
Rapid cooling of a sealed tank can create a vacuum, leading to catastrophic implosion. Manually verify the tank is well-ventilated (manway must be OPEN) before introducing cold water during the final cooldown.
Steam Hazard
During pre-heating, live steam pressure will vent from open side ports and sample valves. Avoid these areas until the tank is up to temperature, then close the valves to prevent chemical spray.

Required PPE:

  • Steel-toe, non-slip footwear
  • Safety glasses or goggles
  • Chemical-resistant gloves and apron
  • Face mask or respirator when handling powdered chemicals

Max Process Temperature: Do not exceed the rated max temperature of your hot water system to protect tank gaskets and seals. Check the Hot Water System specs for your facility's limit.

Cleanliness Acceptance Criteria

A tank is only considered clean and ready for a new batch when it meets all of the following:

  • pH Neutrality: Final rinse water tests at pH 6.5–7.5 (matching incoming city water).
  • Visual Inspection: No visible residue, film, or scale on interior surfaces.
  • Odor: No off-odors or flavor carryover from the previous batch.

CIP Decision Tree

Use the following guidelines to select the appropriate cleaning tier before beginning:

Tier 1
Hot Water Flush Only Routine turnover between same-product batches. Tank held product for less than 6 months. No visible residue after inspection.
Tier 2
Detergent Wash Transitioning between different product types. Spirit held in tank for more than 6 months. Visible residue remains after Tier 1 flush.
Tier 3
Acid Rinse / Passivation Following any tank maintenance (welding, plumbing, confined space entry). Foreign material found (grease, minerals). Heavy mineral scale present.

Pre-Cleaning: Vapor Purge & Inspection

  1. Isolate Agitator: At the main VFD control panel, press STOP for the specific tank's agitator to ensure it cannot be accidentally activated.
  2. Closed-Tank Vapor Purge: Do not open the manway yet. Run Cold City Water through the CIP spray ball to knock down any flammable vapor and dilute remaining product. Continue until the liquid is diluted to below 5% ABV.
  3. Drain & pH Check: Test the pH of the diluted liquid to ensure it meets your municipality's wastewater standard (typically pH 6.0–9.0). Route the drain hose to the trench drain and empty the tank.
  4. Visual Inspection: Once drained and purged, open the bottom manway. Do not break the plane of the manway with your head or torso. Use a flashlight or phone camera to inspect interior walls and spray ball for residue.
  5. Remove and inspect manway gaskets, valves, and removable fittings for buildup or damage.
  6. Verify all hoses, the pump, and the CIP spray ball are clean, connected, and in working order.
PHASE 1 CIP Loop Setup & Pre-Heating — All Tiers

Note: Supply pressure from the hot city water line is typically not sufficient to fully cover the interior of a large tank. A fill-and-recirculate method is required for all tiers.

  1. Assemble CIP Loop: Connect the sight glass to the bottom tank drain. Run a 2" distillery hose from the sight glass to a T-fitting at the AODD pump inlet. Route the other side of this T directly to the trench drain. Connect T-fittings at the pump outlet with butterfly valves on each side as the utility water inlet. Attach a TC sample valve to the manifold. Route a 1.5" hose from the pump outlet to the upper tank manifold, splitting to supply both the CIP spray ball and the fill port arm.
  2. Open Valves: Open all bottom drain valves, outlet valves, side ports, and sample ports to ensure airflow and clear pathways. Keep clear of open ports as the tank heats up — steam will vent.
  3. Line Flush: Open the Hot City Water valve. Spray hot water through the CIP ball and fill port arms for approximately 2 minutes until water runs hot. Close the fill port arm to direct all flow through the spray ball.
  4. Preheat Tank: Fill the tank with Hot City Water to the top of the cone. Shut off water supply.
  5. Recirculate to Temperature: Start the AODD pump and recirculate hot water for 5 minutes to heat the stainless steel.
  6. Verify Temperature: Use an IR thermometer to confirm the tank has reached >140°F. Aim the laser at a non-reflective surface (e.g., a label sticker) for accurate reading. Once hot, close side port and sample valves.
  7. Drain: Dump the pre-rinse water to the trench drain.
Next Step
Tier 1 only: Proceed directly to Phase 4: Cooldown & Final Rinse.
Tier 2: Proceed to Phase 2: Detergent Clean.
Tier 3: Complete Phase 2, then proceed to Phase 3: Acid Rinse.
PHASE 2 Detergent Clean — Tier 2
  1. Dose Water: Fill the tank with Hot City Water to the bottom of the manway.
  2. Add Chemical: Remove the front manway. Weigh out Alkaline Detergent per the dosing benchmarks below (or use the Chemical Dilution Calculator for custom volumes). Add chemical directly to the water through the open manway. Reinstall and tighten the manway.

  3. Wash Cycle: Start the AODD pump and recirculate hot detergent for 30–45 minutes depending on soil load. Open the fill port arm valve for at least 10–15 minutes during this cycle to clean that routing. Briefly open the sample port valve to flush that line.
  4. Safe Chemical Sampling: Never catch a midstream sample from the drain hose. Stop the pump and carefully draw from the manifold sample valve into a glass beaker.
  5. Drain & Rinse: Dump the detergent solution to the trench drain. Flush the tank thoroughly with Hot City Water through all CIP routes.
  6. Verify Neutrality: Use pH test strips on the drain water. Continue rinsing until pH matches incoming city water (~7.0). All detergent must be cleared before proceeding.

PHASE 3 Acid Rinse / Passivation — Tier 3
  1. Ensure Rinsed: Confirm Phase 2 is fully complete and all detergent has been cleared.
  2. Dose Water & Acid: Fill the tank with Hot City Water to the bottom of the manway. Remove the manway and weigh out Citric Acid, Anhydrous per the benchmarks below. Add through the open manway and reseal.

  3. Recirculate & Drain: Start the pump and recirculate through all pathways for 15–20 minutes. Drain the acid solution completely.
  4. Rinse: Rinse thoroughly with Hot City Water until drain water tests pH neutral. If passivating stainless steel, allow the tank to air-dry completely for 24–48 hours before returning to service.
Next Step
Proceed to Phase 4: Cooldown & Final Rinse.
PHASE 4 Cooldown & Final Rinse — All Tiers
Critical — Implosion Risk
Do not shock a hot tank with cold water. Ensure the front manway is OPEN before beginning the cooldown process.
  1. Gradual Cooldown: Begin rinsing by blending Hot City Water and Cold City Water at the utility inlet. Gradually decrease the hot water ratio to safely reduce the internal temperature.
  2. Monitor Temperature: Use an IR thermometer to track the tank surface temperature throughout cooldown.
  3. Final RO Rinse: Once the tank surface drops below 100°F, shut off city water and switch to the RO Water System. Recirculate cold RO water for 10–15 minutes through all pathways to flush hard water residues. This step prevents mineral spotting and scaling. Drain completely.
PHASE 5 Teardown & Record-Keeping
  1. Shut Off Utilities: Turn off the compressed air supply to the AODD pump. Shut off all water supply valves.
  2. Relieve Pressure: Before unclamping any hoses, slightly crack open the highest butterfly valve in the loop to safely bleed off any trapped liquid and air pressure.
  3. Disconnect: Once pressure is relieved, disconnect hoses, drain any remaining standing water in the lines into a bucket, replace the trench drain cover, and return all equipment to the designated storage area.
  4. Log Data: Record the CIP in the facility maintenance logbook. Required fields:
    • Date
    • Operator Name
    • Tank ID(s)
    • Prior Batch ID
    • Tier(s) Performed (Hot Flush / Detergent / Acid)
    • Maximum Temperature Reached (°F)
    • Final Rinse pH
    • Issues / Notes (gasket wear, blockages, anomalies)

Troubleshooting

Residue or Foaming After Detergent Cycle

  • Increase hot city water rinse cycles and duration.
  • Verify correct detergent dosage and temperature — solution must be above 140°F for effective action.

pH Not Neutral After Acid Rinse

  • Extend recirculation time or reduce acid concentration slightly for the next cycle.
  • Ensure adequate hot water rinsing before switching to the cold RO final rinse.

Spray Ball Not Dispersing Evenly

  • Check for clogs or mineral buildup in the spray ball assembly. Disassemble and soak in citric acid solution if needed.
  • Increase pump flow rate via the Compressed Air System regulator if spray coverage is insufficient.

Tank Not Reaching Temperature

  • Check the Hot Water System setpoint and recovery status. Allow more recovery time if the system has been in heavy use.
  • Ensure the tank is closed (side ports and sample valves shut) during recirculation to retain heat.
