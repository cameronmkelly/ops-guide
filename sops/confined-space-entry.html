SOP: Confined Space Entry Program

Tiered permit-required confined space entry procedures for facility processing tanks, IBC totes, and the emergency generator enclosure.

Purpose: Establish a safe, compliant, and operationally practical program for identifying, evaluating, and controlling entry into permit-required confined spaces (PRCS) at this facility. This SOP establishes a three-tier entry model scaled to the realities of a small-batch craft distillery and satisfies the requirements of 29 CFR 1910.146 and Oregon OSHA OAR 437-002-0146.

Scope: Applies to all employees and contractors performing or supporting any entry into a facility-classified confined space, including the four stainless steel processing tanks (PT01–PT04), all facility-operated IBC totes, and the emergency generator enclosure.

Operational Model: This facility typically operates with one or two people on-site. Any confined space entry requires a minimum of two people: the authorized entrant and a dedicated attendant. No employee may enter a confined space alone under any circumstances.

⚠️ Key Safety Rules

Never Enter Alone
A dedicated attendant must be present outside the space for the entire duration of any entry. The attendant may not leave the post for any reason while an entrant is inside.
Avoid Entry When Possible
If a task can be accomplished without breaking the plane of the opening — by using a mirror, camera, long-handled tool, or by repositioning the vessel — that method must be used instead of entry.
OSHA Definition of Entry
Any part of the body breaking the plane of the confined space opening constitutes entry, including arm-reach sampling. All PRCS requirements apply from the moment of entry.

Entry Decision

Select the appropriate tier before any entry. When in doubt, escalate to the next tier. Facility employees may perform Tier 1 and Tier 2 entries. Tier 3 entries are contractor-only — Employees do not enter under Tier 3 conditions.

Tier 1
Employee Manway Inspection Post-CIP visual inspection of processing tank (PT01–PT04) interiors. Head and shoulders may enter bottom manway for sensory assessment. No tools, no sustained work inside. Minimum team: 1 employee (entrant) + 1 attendant.
Tier 2
Employee Limited IBC Access Limited access to any facility-operated IBC tote to retrieve an object that cannot be reached from outside after repositioning. Minimum team: 1 employee (entrant) + 1 attendant.
Tier 3
Contractor Full PRCS Entry Any entry beyond Tier 1 or Tier 2 scope — welding, inspection, repair, or maintenance inside any facility vessel or the generator enclosure. Contractor issues their own Entry Permit. Facility acts as host employer and issues the Hazard Acknowledgment form.
TIER 1 Employee Manway Inspection — Processing Tanks (PT01–PT04)

Scope: Post-CIP visual and sensory assessment. The entrant may insert their head and shoulders through the bottom manway to inspect interior surfaces. No sustained work inside the tank.

Prerequisites — All Must Be Confirmed

  • CIP Tier 1 — Hot Water Flush completed. Tank fully drained.
  • All CIP flexible hoses (cold water, hot water, RO water, compressed air) disconnected and removed from the vessel.
  • Agitator LOTO applied at external motor disconnect and verified (zero-energy state confirmed).
  • No visible vapor, no ethanol odor at the manway opening, no visible liquid inside.
  • Building LEL monitor not in alarm.
  • Attendant in position outside the space. Verbal brief given and logged.
No 4-Gas Meter Required for Tier 1
Because this facility does not conduct active fermentation, there is no CO or CO₂ source inside processing tanks after a hot water flush. Sensory assessment (no visible vapor, no sharp ethanol odor) is the acceptable pre-entry check for Tier 1 and Tier 2. If any doubt exists, ventilate and wait, or escalate to Tier 3.

Procedure

  1. Log the entry. Record date, time, asset ID, purpose, entrant initials, and attendant name in the Confined Space Entry Log before entry begins.
  2. Position attendant. Attendant takes position at the manway with a clear line of sight. Communication plan confirmed (voice contact throughout).
  3. Open manway. Slowly open the bottom manway. Pause and assess: no odor, no visible vapor, no heat from the opening.
  4. Insert head and shoulders only. Using a flashlight, inspect all interior surfaces, spray ball, and agitator shaft for residue, damage, or foreign material. Do not proceed further into the tank.
  5. Exit and close. Remove from the manway, close and torque the manway cover, log findings in the Entry Log.
  6. Remove LOTO. After the manway is secured and entrant is fully clear, release the agitator LOTO per the LOTO SOP.
Abort Conditions — Exit Immediately If:
  • Attendant signals to exit
  • Any odor, heat, or unusual condition detected inside
  • Building LEL alarm activates
  • Any entrant feels lightheaded, short of breath, or unusual
TIER 2 Employee Limited IBC Access

Scope: Limited torso-and-arm access into any facility-operated IBC tote to retrieve an object that cannot be reached from outside after repositioning the tote. Applies to all current and future facility-operated IBCs.

Reposition First
Before any Tier 2 entry, attempt to reposition or tilt the IBC to retrieve the object without entry. Entry is only permitted if repositioning is not feasible.

Prerequisites — All Must Be Confirmed

  • All Tier 1 prerequisites confirmed (sensory assessment, no vapor, no odor, no visible liquid).
  • All CIP flexible hoses and the dip tube assembly disconnected and removed from the IBC.
  • IBC fully drained. No liquid visible inside.
  • Building LEL monitor not in alarm.
  • Attendant in position outside the space. Verbal brief given and logged.

Procedure

  1. Log the entry. Record in the Confined Space Entry Log. Note that LOTO is N/A (IBCs have no permanent power).
  2. Position attendant. Attendant takes position at the IBC opening with a clear line of sight and continuous verbal contact.
  3. Open IBC lid. Open the top lid. Pause and assess: no odor, no heat, no vapor.
  4. Limited entry. Entrant may lower head, shoulders, and arms into the IBC only as far as required to retrieve the object. Duration must be the minimum necessary.
  5. Exit and secure. Exit the IBC, secure the lid, log findings and duration in the Entry Log.
Abort Conditions — Same as Tier 1
Exit immediately if the attendant signals, any unusual condition is detected, or the entrant experiences any physical symptom.
TIER 3 Contractor Full PRCS Entry

Scope: Any entry beyond Tier 1 or Tier 2 scope, including welding, inspection, repair, or any maintenance task requiring sustained work inside a confined space. Applies to all facility confined spaces including the emergency generator enclosure. Employees do not enter under Tier 3 conditions.

Before Contractor Arrival — Facility Responsibilities

  1. Issue Hazard Acknowledgment. Provide the contractor with the completed Confined Space Hazard Acknowledgment form covering all hazards applicable to the entry.
  2. Coordinate LOTO. Perform facility-side energy isolation (tank drain, CIP Tier 1 flush, agitator LOTO or generator LOTO) before contractor arrival, per the LOTO SOP.
  3. Confirm contractor program. Verify the contractor has their own written PRCS program, trained personnel, atmospheric testing equipment, rescue provisions, and Entry Permit authority.

Day of Entry — Host Employer Obligations

  1. Brief the contractor’s Entry Supervisor on all hazards in the space, including any conditions that may have changed since the Hazard Acknowledgment was issued.
  2. Inform the contractor of any facility LOTO devices already applied and coordinate dual-lock procedures where applicable.
  3. Maintain open communication with the contractor throughout the entry.
  4. Debrief with the contractor Entry Supervisor upon completion. Note any new or unexpected hazards discovered.
  5. Retain a copy of the contractor’s completed Entry Permit for a minimum of one year.
Who Issues the Entry Permit for Tier 3?
The contractor’s Entry Supervisor issues the Entry Permit under the contractor’s own program, per 29 CFR 1910.146(c)(9)(iv). The facility issues the Hazard Acknowledgment form (the host employer document) and retains a copy of the contractor’s permit. The facility Entry Permit template may be provided to contractors who do not have their own form.

Required Roles — All Entries

RoleWho Fills ItKey Duties
Entry Supervisor Operations Manager (Tier 1/2); Contractor Entry Supervisor (Tier 3) Verify prerequisites met; authorize entry; cancel permit if conditions change; conduct post-entry debrief
Attendant Any trained employee or qualified contractor attendant Maintain continuous contact with entrant; monitor for hazards; summon rescue if needed; never leave post while entrant is inside
Authorized Entrant Any trained employee (Tier 1/2); contractor entrant (Tier 3) Know the hazards; use required PPE; maintain communication with attendant; exit immediately when ordered

Permit Duration and Cancellation

Entry permits are valid for a single calendar day (midnight) only. A new permit (or new Entry Log entry for Tier 1/2) is required for each separate entry event. Permits must be cancelled — and all entrants accounted for — before the entry is considered closed. Cancelled permits are retained for one year minimum.

Immediate Cancellation Required If:
  • Conditions change such that new hazards arise or existing hazards worsen
  • An entrant shows signs of distress
  • A fire alarm or facility emergency is declared
  • Communication with the entrant is lost

Atmospheric Testing Reference

Atmospheric testing for Tier 3 entries must be performed by a competent person using a calibrated multi-gas detector before entry and at regular intervals during entry. Acceptable entry conditions per 29 CFR 1910.146(b):

ParameterAcceptable RangeAction if Out of Range
Oxygen (O₂)19.5%–23.5%Ventilate; do not enter until stable
Flammable gas / vapor (LEL)<10% LELVentilate; do not enter until stable
Carbon monoxide (CO)<35 ppmVentilate; identify source; do not enter
Hydrogen sulfide (H₂S)<1 ppmEvacuate area; do not enter
Facility Does Not Own a 4-Gas Meter
The facility does not currently own atmospheric testing equipment. For Tier 1 and Tier 2, sensory assessment is used (no vapor, no odor, building LEL not in alarm). For Tier 3, the contractor is required to provide calibrated atmospheric testing equipment as part of their PRCS program. In situations where atmospheric testing by the facility is warranted (e.g., post-incident, uncertain conditions), a 4-gas monitor may be rented from a local safety supplier.

Records

  • Confined Space Entry Log — All Tier 1 and Tier 2 entries are logged here. Stored in the operations records. This log also serves as the simplified permit record for Tier 1/2 entries.
  • Confined Space Hazard Acknowledgment — Completed and signed before each Tier 3 entry. Retained with the corresponding Entry Permit copy for one year minimum.
  • Confined Space Entry Permit — Template available for Tier 3 entries. Contractor’s own permit is acceptable; a copy must be retained by the facility.
  • Annual review. This SOP and the confined space inventory must be reviewed at least annually and updated whenever physical changes are made to the facility or equipment.
