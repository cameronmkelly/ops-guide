SOP: Proofing Operations

Diluting higher-proof spirit with RO water to reach a target bottling or transfer proof.

Purpose: Define the process and scientific principles for reducing spirit proof by adding RO Water. This document establishes guidelines for recognizing and mitigating proofing challenges, including louching and saponification.

Scope: Applies to all employees involved in proofing spirit for processing, barreling, or bottling.

⚠️ Safety & PPE Requirements

  • Safety glasses or goggles — protect against splashes
  • Nitrile or chemical-resistant gloves
  • Non-slip footwear
  • Ensure adequate ventilation in all proofing areas
Water Quality
Always use properly demineralized water from the RO Water System. High-mineral content in proofing water can cause irreversible saponification (cloudiness/sediment).

Key Concepts

Louching (Chill Haze)

Louching occurs when certain compounds precipitate out of solution as water concentration increases, causing a cloudy appearance. It may be reversible by increasing temperature. Common causes include grain oils, botanical oils, and fatty acids from fermentation.

Saponification

A process in which salts or minerals in dilution water react with fatty acids, forming insoluble compounds. Unlike louching, saponification is usually irreversible. Prevention requires exclusive use of demineralized RO water and gradual dilution steps.

Dilution Risk Zones

Low Risk
95% – 57% ABVRecommended reduction: 10–20% per step.
Moderate
57% – 52% ABVRecommended reduction: 3–5% per step.
High Risk
52% – 45% ABVRecommended reduction: 2–3% per step. Monitor closely for haze.
Moderate
44% – 40% ABVRecommended reduction: 2–5% per step.

Procedure

  1. Confirm current proof using the Alcohol Meter. Calculate the target water addition using the dilution calculator or facility formula.
  2. Add RO water slowly and evenly. Never dump a large volume of water directly into concentrated spirit.
  3. Agitate thoroughly between each addition to ensure even mixing and prevent localized ABV gradients.
  4. Re-check proof after each addition step. Do not proceed to the next step until the reading has stabilized.
  5. If haze forms, increase temperature slightly and assess whether compounds return to solution (louching) or remain (saponification).
  6. Log all proofing adjustments in the compliance system: final ABV, reduction steps, water source, and any anomalies.
