SOP: Bottling Line Operation

Pre-operation setup, production run, and teardown for the manual bottle filling line.

Purpose: Establish a standardized procedure for operating the manual bottle filler, including sanitary setup, TTB fill tolerance compliance, and end-of-run teardown and CIP.

Scope: Applies to all operators responsible for bottling operations.

⚠️ Safety & PPE Requirements

  • Steel-toe, non-slip footwear
  • Safety glasses — protect against glass breakage and alcohol splashes
  • Cut-resistant gloves during bottle handling and glass breakage cleanup
  • Ensure ventilation is active throughout the production run

Phase 1: Pre-Operation Sanitary Verification

  1. Inspect the Bottle Filler for residual product, mineral buildup, or gasket wear from the previous run. Address any issues before proceeding.
  2. Flush all product contact surfaces with RO Water, then sanitize using No-Rinse Sanitizer at the appropriate concentration. Allow to drain.
  3. Confirm all fill tubes, valves, and gaskets are properly seated.

Phase 2: TTB Fill Calibration

Compliance Requirement
Fill volumes must comply with TTB regulations (27 CFR 19.356). Calibrate the filler at the start of each production run and after any interruption exceeding 30 minutes.
  1. Fill a sample bottle from each fill head. Weigh and calculate fill volume using the fill-by-weight method: Net Weight (g) ÷ Density (g/mL) = Volume (mL)
  2. Verify fill volume is within tolerance for the declared bottle size. Adjust fill tubes as needed until all heads are in compliance.
  3. Document calibration readings, adjustments made, and operator initials.

Phase 3: Production Run

  1. Begin filling at a controlled pace. Verify fill level visually on the first 5–10 bottles of each run.
  2. Periodically re-weigh bottles throughout the run to confirm fill compliance is maintained.
  3. If a bottle breaks: stop immediately, contain and dispose of glass safely. Do not resume until the fill station is thoroughly inspected and cleared of glass fragments.
  4. Log production data: date, product, lot number, total bottles filled, operator initials.

Phase 4: Teardown & CIP

  1. Flush the filler with RO water to remove residual product.
  2. Perform a full CIP cycle using Alkaline Detergent solution at the concentration specified for this equipment type. Rinse thoroughly to pH neutral.
  3. Final rinse with No-Rinse Sanitizer. Allow to drain — do not rinse off.
  4. Disassemble fill heads, inspect gaskets, and store in clean condition.
