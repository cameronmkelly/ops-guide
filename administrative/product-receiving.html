Product & Material Receiving

Inspection, logging, and storage procedures for all incoming shipments.

Purpose: Establish consistent procedures for receiving, inspecting, and storing incoming materials — including raw materials, chemicals, supplies, and equipment. Proper receiving practices protect product quality, ensure chemical safety, and support compliance record-keeping.

General Receiving Procedure

  1. Inspect Before Signing: Inspect every delivery for visible damage before signing the delivery receipt. Note any damage on the bill of lading and photograph if applicable. Do not accept a damaged shipment of chemicals without first confirming with the vendor.
  2. Verify Against Purchase Order: Confirm item descriptions, quantities, and lot numbers match the purchase order. Report discrepancies to the vendor immediately.
  3. Check Documentation: Confirm that SDS files are current for any chemical order. Request updated SDS if the formulation has changed since the last order.
  4. Log the Receipt: Record all received items in the facility receiving log: date, vendor, item description, quantity, lot number, and condition.
  5. Route to Designated Storage: Move materials to the correct storage area immediately. Do not leave chemicals unattended in the receiving area.

Chemical Receiving — Additional Requirements

Segregation Required
Oxidizing chemicals (e.g., alkaline detergents) and acid chemicals (e.g., citric acid) must be stored in separate, clearly labeled areas. Do not store incompatible chemicals in proximity.
  • Verify that the received chemical matches the product name, concentration, and manufacturer on the purchase order — do not assume.
  • Mark received date on all containers for FIFO rotation.
  • Add new stock behind existing stock.
  • Update the chemical inventory log.

Equipment & Parts Receiving

  • Inspect all equipment and parts for shipping damage before installing or storing.
  • Retain all packing materials until the item is confirmed functional.
  • File manuals, warranties, and spec sheets in the relevant equipment note in this guide.
