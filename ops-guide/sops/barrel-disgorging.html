SOP: Barrel Disgorging

Safe transfer of aged spirit from barrels using an AODD pump and cartridge filtration.

Purpose: Establish safe and efficient procedures for transferring spirit from barrels using an air-operated diaphragm pump, spirit hoses, a stainless steel barrel suction tube, and a cartridge filter housing. This procedure ensures product integrity, operator safety, and regulatory compliance.

Scope: Applies to all operators responsible for barrel disgorging within the facility.

⚠️ Safety & PPE Requirements

Static Discharge — Critical
High-proof alcohol flowing through hoses generates static electricity. Grounding cables and clamps must be attached to the pump and all metal components before beginning any transfer.
  • Steel-toe, non-slip footwear — required at all times on the production floor
  • Safety glasses — required; protect against high-proof alcohol splashes
  • Nitrile or chemical-resistant gloves
  • Hearing protection if operating near the AODD pump
  • Ensure adequate ventilation to prevent flammable vapor accumulation

Phase 1: Pre-Operation Setup

  1. Line Flush: Flush all spirit transfer hoses and the AODD pump with hot water from the Hot Water System to remove any residual buildup. Follow with a final rinse using RO Water.
  2. Equipment Check: Inspect all hoses, fittings, and the filter housing for wear, cracking, or improper seating. Verify the receiving vessel is clean, dry, and labeled.
  3. Barrel Inspection: Check the barrel for structural damage or active leaking before positioning.
  4. Grounding: Attach grounding cables and clamps to the pump and all metal components. Confirm hoses are free of kinks.
  5. Assemble Transfer Line: Connect the barrel suction tube → inlet hose → AODD pump → cartridge filter housing → receiving vessel. Secure all tri-clamp fittings with gaskets.

Phase 2: Sample Collection

  1. Before disgorging, extract a sample from each barrel to test for proof and quality assurance.
  2. Use the Alcohol Meter to record the proof measurement.
  3. Label each sample container immediately with: barrel number, lot number, date, proof measurement, and sensory notes.

Phase 3: Transfer

  1. Position the barrel on a secure rack or cradle and remove the bung using a bung puller. Allow the barrel to vent for a moment to equalize pressure.
  2. Insert the suction tube, ensuring it reaches near the barrel bottom without creating excessive suction against the wood.
  3. Slowly activate the Compressed Air supply to the AODD pump to begin the transfer.
  4. Monitor flow rate to prevent cavitation or excessive foaming. Observe all connections continuously for leaks.
  5. Once the barrel is empty, shut off the pump. Remove the suction tube and drain residual liquid into the receiving vessel.
  6. Seal and label the receiving vessel. Inspect the empty barrel for residual liquid, then secure for storage or repurposing.

Phase 4: Post-Operation Cleanup

  1. Flush all spirit hoses and the AODD pump with RO water immediately after use.
  2. Run RO water through the cartridge filter housing to clear residual product and sediment.
  3. Inspect all equipment, replace worn gaskets, and return to designated storage.

Record-Keeping

All barrel disgorging must be logged in the facility compliance system. Required fields: barrel number, lot number, proof before and after, volume transferred, date, operator initials.
