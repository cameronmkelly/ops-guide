Citric Acid, Anhydrous

Food-grade acid for Tier 3 CIP acid rinsing and stainless steel passivation.

Purpose: Used for Phase 3 (Tier 3) CIP acid rinsing to neutralize alkaline residues and remove mineral scale. Also used for passivation of new or recently repaired stainless steel equipment to restore the protective chromium oxide layer.

Dosing & Application

Always Verify Against Calculator
Use the Chemical Dilution Calculator for any volume not listed below.

Standard acid rinse dose: 100 g/gal (~2.6% by weight). Acceptable range: 2.0–3.0%.
Passivation dose: 4.0% by weight. After passivation, allow the tank to air-dry completely for 24–48 hours before returning to service.

Storage & Handling

  • Store in a cool, well-ventilated area in tightly sealed containers.
  • Keep away from oxidizers, strong acids, strong bases, and ignition sources.
  • Minimize dust generation when transferring or mixing dry powder — dust may be combustible and can generate static electricity.

Material Compatibility

Compatible: Stainless steel (304, 316), PTFE, polyethylene, glass.

Incompatible: Strong oxidizers (nitric acid, sulfuric acid), strong bases. Do not mix with the Alkaline Detergent directly — always rinse equipment to pH neutral before introducing citric acid.

Safety & First Aid

Eye Irritant
Can cause severe eye irritation. Wear goggles when handling powder or solutions. Airborne dust may be combustible.
  • Eye contact: Flush with water for 15–20 minutes, removing contact lenses if present.
  • Skin contact: Wash thoroughly with soap and water.
  • Inhalation: Move to fresh air.
  • Ingestion: Do not induce vomiting. Rinse mouth; give small sips of water.
